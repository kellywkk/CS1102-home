What is E-Banking?

E-banking (electronic/online banking) enables customers to conduct financial transactions through the internet, mobile apps, and ATMs. Primary services include:

  • Account balance checks
  • Bill payments
  • Fund transfers
  • Credit card management
  • Order checks

Key Benefits

Time Saving

People can transfer and receive money instantly using the internet or mobile apps, which is time saving. This is different from waiting in the long queue for money transfer and receive.

Faster transction

The important benefit of e-banking is information distribution quickly about the worth of money in anyplace anytime with a mouse click. People can transfer money anyplace anytime using the e-banking system that reflect e-banking provide a faster transavtions.


Current Challenges

Usage Barriers

Elderly and non-tech-savvy users often struggle with digital interfaces and authentication methods like face recognition.

Security Concerns

Many customers worry about hacking risks and unauthorized access. Banks can implement two-factor authentication and encryption to address these concerns.


Conclusion

E-banking has changed the way for people to manage their finances, for example individuals and businesses. They provide convenience and efficiency within the e-banking transactions. They conduct transactions and provide the number of financial services using the e-banking systems. However, there are some challenges associated with usages and security issues. Therefore, they need to continue support and educate people, for example online tutorials, workshops and customer service centers.
